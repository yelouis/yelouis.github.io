About Me

Hello there! I am sophomore at Carleton College double majoring in Computer Science and Economics.

I am passionate about pursuing a career in software engineering, data analytics, and/or entrepreneurship. I started as self-taught programmer and expanded my computer science knowledge through my high school and college education. This has lead to multiple software internships and research opportunities. At Carleton, I have coded 3 research projects about machine learning. Apart from hiding behind a computer, I have had the privilege of being an original lead engineer of the JuiceBox Project, which has evolved into a nonprofit organization, Shine, raising over $60,000 in donations and operating across multiple states. I also love pursuing entrepreneurial endeavors, and have recently cofounded a small collegiate bracelet engraving company.

I am originally from Singapore but grew up in Portland, Oregon, where I attended Catlin Gabel. At Catlin, I served as the student body Vice President, led the Boys Varsity Tennis Team, and volunteered on the InvenTeam to engineer solutions to societal problems. In my free time, you can find me practicing piano, playing tennis, or weightlifting.
